Methodology & governance — built to be the benchmark

Designed to IOSCO benchmark principles from day one, because indices that get written into supply contracts are audited, not just trusted.

01Volume-weighted, transaction-only. Indications and offers are tracked but never enter a published price — only transacted prints.
02Minimum 3 independent contributors per published figure. Below threshold, the price is suppressed and marked "thin" — never estimated.
03Outlier dampening, not deletion. Prints beyond 2σ of the rolling median are downweighted with the action logged to an immutable audit trail.
04Anti-signaling controls. No raw competitor prints visible; aggregation thresholds and time delays on identifiable data, reviewed by antitrust counsel.
05Published revision policy. Corrections are versioned, timestamped, and announced — never silent.
06Independent advisory board of named traders, mill economists, and a methodology auditor reviews quarterly.
07Anti-circularity rule. Prints priced by index-linked contract formulas are recorded but excluded from assessment — an index that ingests prices set by itself stops measuring the market.
08Full print specification. Every print captures basis (FOB/delivered), tally (RL 8/20', 8/16', specified), shipment window, quantity class, duty inclusion, and payment terms — a price without its terms isn't comparable.
09Daily settlement. The live board drives trading decisions; one official 3:30 p.m. PT settlement print per day gives contracts and hedges a citable close. Settlements are final — never retroactively changed by late data.
10Annual open consultation. Methodology is reviewed yearly with public comment; material changes are announced by pricing notice with a versioned revision history.
